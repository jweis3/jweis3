## 🌱 About Me
  Welcome to my Github! My name is **Jason Weis**, and I'm a single cell and spatial biologist with 8+ years of developing and anlyzing complex multi-omic technologies. I have applied much of this experitse within the immuno-oncology space, focusing on early detection and biomarker development for lung cancer as well as designing the next generation of tunable CAR-T cells. 

### 🎓 **Education:** 

I'm currently a [Genetics and Genomics PhD Candidate](https://www.bumc.bu.edu/gpgg/) at the [Boston University Chobanian & Avedesian School of Medicine](https://www.bumc.bu.edu/camed/). My research has focused on leveraging both single cell and spatial transcirptomics to identify, validate, and enhance biomarkers for early stage lung cancers. I completed my [B.S. in Biomedical Engineering](https://bme.gatech.edu/bme/) from [Georgia Tech](https://www.gatech.edu/) where my research entailed creating thermally regulated genetic constructs to improve safety of CAR-T based therapeutics.  

### 🧬 **Background:** 
Prior to my PhD, I held Research Associate positions at [ReadCoor](https://www.genomeweb.com/sequencing/10x-genomics-acquire-readcoor-350m-cartana-412m), and subsequently [10x Genomics](https://www.10xgenomics.com/). At ReadCoor I contrubuted heavily to implementing both a novel cell morphology detection system and the co-detection of protein and RNA targets within the same tissue section. During my time at 10x, I primarily focused on optimizing sample prepartion conditions which enabled multi-omic detection of both proteins and transcripts. These products are now commercially available as [10x Xenium in situ](https://www.10xgenomics.com/platforms/xenium) and [Xenium Protein](https://www.10xgenomics.com/products/xenium-protein)!
